Business model

A trust-based model, built to outlast quarters.

Reep's revenue is designed around a simple constraint: the platform must still exist in 50 years. Every line of the model reflects that.

Value creation

We sell certainty, not storage.

Cloud storage is a commodity. What Reep actually sells is the certainty that what you store will reach the right person, at the right time, in a usable form — something no general-purpose cloud provider is built to guarantee.

For individuals

Peace of mind that nothing — financial, legal, or personal — vanishes when life happens.

For estate professionals

A modern, defensible system to offer high-net-worth clients alongside traditional estate planning.

For beneficiaries

A guided, dignified handoff — instead of a scavenger hunt through a deceased relative's accounts.

Revenue streams

Tiered subscriptions, plus a B2B2C partner channel.

B2C

The Essential Vault

Password transfer and basic legal document storage. $5/month or $50/year.

B2C · MOST POPULAR

The Legacy Pro

Expanded storage for media archives, crypto-management tools, and video messages. $15/month.

B2C · ONE-TIME

The Eternal Plan

A perpetual hosting fee that maintains your vault for 50+ years after death, for time-delayed messages.

B2B2C · PARTNERSHIP INTEGRATION

Reep partners with estate planning law firms and wealth management groups, who offer Reep as a value-added service to their high-net-worth clients — a distribution channel that comes pre-qualified and pre-trusted.

Scalability

Storage is cheap. Trust compounds.

Encrypted storage has a low and falling marginal cost. The real scaling lever is trust — each generation of beneficiaries who experience a smooth transfer becomes a future depositor themselves.

Low marginal cost per vault

Encrypted blob storage scales sub-linearly with users.

Regional data centers

Expand jurisdiction by jurisdiction without re-architecting.

Generational compounding

Beneficiaries become depositors; trust becomes distribution.

Competitive moat

Hard to copy, because it's hard to trust quickly.

Zero-knowledge architecture

A genuine zero-knowledge system takes years to build correctly and is expensive to fake convincingly.

LEVS, proven over time

A verification system earns credibility only through a track record of correct calls — first-mover advantage compounds here.

Compliance as moat

RUFADAA and GDPR-native design from day one is a multi-year head start competitors must replicate.

Estate-partner relationships

Law firm and wealth-manager trust takes years of relationship-building to earn — and to switch away from.
